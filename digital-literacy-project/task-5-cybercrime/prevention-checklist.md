# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist for College Students

## General Online Safety

1. Always check the URL of a website before entering any personal information. Official Indian government sites end in .gov.in — be suspicious of anything else.

2. Never click on links in unsolicited emails or SMS messages, even if they appear to be from banks, scholarship portals, or government bodies. Go directly to the official website by typing the address yourself.

3. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all important accounts — email, social media, and banking apps.

4. Use a strong, unique password for each account. A strong password has at least 8 characters and includes letters, numbers, and symbols. Use a password manager if needed.

5. Keep your phone, laptop, and apps updated. Security patches in updates fix known vulnerabilities that attackers exploit.

6. Do not use public Wi-Fi (college canteen, railway stations, cafes) for banking, UPI transactions, or logging into sensitive accounts without a VPN.

## UPI and Financial Safety (India-Specific)

7. NEVER share your UPI PIN, OTP, or mPIN with anyone — not even someone claiming to be from your bank or NPCI. Banks never ask for these details.

8. Be aware that scammers use "collect requests" on UPI apps to trick you into authorising payments by pretending it is a refund or prize. Sending money and receiving money are different actions — verify every transaction carefully before entering your PIN.

## Reporting

9. If you suspect you have been targeted by a cybercrime or have lost money online, report it immediately:
   - National Cyber Crime Portal: cybercrime.gov.in
   - Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 (available 24x7, toll-free)
   - The faster you report, the higher the chance of recovering lost funds.

---

**Remember:** When in doubt, don't click, don't share, and don't pay. Report to cybercrime.gov.in or call 1930.
