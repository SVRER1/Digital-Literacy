# Task 5 – Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing

## Cybercrime Type: Phishing

## Case Study

### Background
Phishing is one of the most common forms of cybercrime in India, affecting thousands of students and professionals every year. The following is a realistic scenario based on commonly reported phishing patterns targeting college students.

### Scenario: The Fake Scholarship Email

Riya, a first-year engineering student in Bhopal, received an email that appeared to be from the National Scholarship Portal (NSP). The email had an official-looking logo and stated that she had been selected for a merit-based scholarship worth Rs. 50,000. To claim the amount, she was asked to click a link and fill in her details immediately, as the offer would expire in 24 hours.

### How It Happened – Step by Step

1. The attacker registered a domain name closely resembling the official site (e.g., nsp-scholarships.net instead of scholarships.gov.in).
2. A mass phishing email was sent to thousands of college email IDs sourced from public university portals.
3. The email used urgency ("offer expires in 24 hours") and authority (official logos, formal language) to pressure the recipient.
4. When Riya clicked the link, she was taken to a fake website that looked identical to the NSP portal.
5. She entered her name, date of birth, Aadhaar number, and bank account details.
6. The attackers used this information to attempt identity fraud and attempted an unauthorised bank transfer.

### Who Is Usually Targeted
Students, job seekers, and first-time internet users who may not be familiar with spotting fake websites or recognising phishing indicators.

### Consequences
- Financial loss (money transferred out of the account)
- Identity theft (Aadhaar and personal details misused)
- Psychological distress and loss of trust in digital services
- Difficulty reversing fraudulent transactions without prompt reporting

### Source / Reference
Based on patterns reported by CERT-In (Indian Computer Emergency Response Team) and cybercrime.gov.in awareness resources.
